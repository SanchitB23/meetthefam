Three rings, one heart. The MeetMyFamily mark in full system — construction, clearspace, color, type, lockups, and the rules that hold them all together.

01

Construction

The knot is built on a 30-unit grid. Three identical circles, equilateral triangle of centers, a single accent dot at the centroid. Nothing else — that's the discipline.

1
Three circles, equal radius
r = 2u. Identical strokes, no variation.
2
Equilateral triangle of centers
Side = 2.4r. Top center plumb above centroid.
3
Stroke at 0.18r
Round caps. Scales with the mark.
4
Heart at the centroid
Filled accent dot, radius = 0.33r.

07

Do's & don'ts

The mark is generous — these rules are about preserving its silence.

✓Use as drawn
Two-color, rings open, heart at center.
✓Invert on dark
Rings become cream, heart stays terracotta.
✕Don't recolor
Only Ink / Cream / Terracotta. No exceptions.
✕Don't fill the rings
The overlap is the meaning. Fill kills it.
✕Don't stretch
Rings must remain circular. Scale uniformly.
✕Don't rotate
The triangle of centers must stay upright.
✕Don't drop-shadow
The mark sits on the surface, never above it.
✕Don't bulk the stroke
Stroke ratio is locked at 0.18r.
